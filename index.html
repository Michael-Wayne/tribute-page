Shelly-Ann Fraser-Pryce

The greatest sprinter the world has ever seen.

Here's a timeline of Shelly-Ann's journey so far:

  • 1986

    Born in the district of Waterhouse in Kingston, Jamaica

  • 2002

    Ran 100 metres in 11.57 seconds at age 16 while at the Wolmer's Trust High School for Girls

  • 2007

    Improved her best to 11.31 seconds while at the University of Technology and placed fifth at the national championships

  • 2008

    Won gold in the 100 metres at the Beijing Olympics with a new personal best of 10.78 seconds

  • 2009

    Won gold in the 100 metres at the World Champtionships in Berlin in another personal best of 10.73 seconds

  • 2012

    Got married to long time boyfriend, Jason Pryce

    Later that year at the London Games, she became the third woman to repeat as Olympic 100 metre champion, with another personal best (10.70 seconds)

    Competed in the 200 metres for the first time at a major championship and came away with the silver

  • 2013

    Won the women's 100 metre final in 10.71 seconds at the World Champtionships in Moscow

    Four days later Fraser-Pryce captured gold in the 200 metres (22.17 seconds), becoming the third woman to claim such a dash double

    She then anchored Jamaica's team to secure gold in the women's 4 x 100 metre relay

    At season's end the International Association of Athletics Federations named Fraser-Pryce its 2013 Woman Athlete of the Year

  • 2015

    Won gold as a member of Jamaica's 4 x 100-metre relay team and in the 100 metre event at the World Champtionships in Beijing, becoming the first woman to win three 100 metre golds in world championships history

  • 2016

    At the 2016 Rio de Janeiro Olympic Games, she battled through a persistent toe injury to take the silver medal in the 4 x 100 metre relay and the bronze in the 100 metre sprint

  • 2017

    Fraser-Pryce announced that she was pregnant—she gave birth to a boy in August—and she did not return to competition until the following year

  • 2019

    At the World Championships in Doha, she claimed her fourth title in the 100 metres and was a member of the gold-winning 4 x 100 metre relay team

  • 2021

    At the 2020 Tokyo Olymic Games (held in 2021 due to the COVID-19 Pandemic), she won silver in the 100 metres and gold as part of the 4 x 100 metre relay team

  • 2022

    At the World Championships in Eugene Oregon, Fraser-Pryce won a historic 5th world title in the 100 metres in a time of 10.67 seconds

"When it comes to Jamaica, we always say we bleed black, green and gold. Here we are as Black women, and young Black women, just making sure that we put our country on the map... A lot of our athletes are coming from nothing. We have to sacrifice so much to represent our country, to show other young girls that it doesn't matter if we don't have the fancy stadium or the fancy tracks that everybody else trains on. We can achieve greatness right here. Right at home."

—Shelly-Ann Fraser-Pryce
